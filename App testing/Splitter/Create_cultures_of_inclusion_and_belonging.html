Create cultures of inclusion and belonging

Why it matters

A culture of inclusion and belonging ensures that all employees feel valued, respected, and empowered to contribute their best work. Employees who feel a strong sense of belonging are more productive, resilient to the stressors of work, and less likely to leave their organizations. When workplaces actively foster inclusion, they create an environment where employees from all backgrounds can thrive.

Among aid workers specifically, social support has been associated with reductions in depression, psychological distress, and burnout. Inclusive workplace culture also counteracts harmful cultural archetypes such as machismo or martyr culture, which act as barriers to mental-health-seeking behavior. Creating cultures of inclusion and belonging is a particularly cost-effective way to improve employee wellness.

Recommendations

Promote social connection

Encourage socialization and unstructured interactions between employees by setting aside free time during the workday and dedicating spaces for non-work activities. For remote teams, make sure to leave room for jokes, pets, and other social interactions on team chats and message boards.

Model inclusive behavior

Encourage leaders and managers to model inclusive behaviors by actively engaging with all employees, fostering open dialogue, and addressing biases.

Create a psychologically safe work environment and promote collaboration and open communication even in professional scenarios.

Work with the DEIA Committee or ERGs to social events, bringing together all levels of the organization to celebrate the diverse cultures represented at the company.
