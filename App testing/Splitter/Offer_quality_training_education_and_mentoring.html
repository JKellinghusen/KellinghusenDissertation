Offer quality training, education, and mentoring

Why it matters

Training, education, and mentoring help employees stay engaged, develop their skills, and contribute more effectively to the organization. Companies that prioritize learning and development (L&D) see increased employee retention, higher job satisfaction, and improved productivity. However, most companies do not offer any continuous L&D programs, leaving vast potential on the table.

Among aid organizations, continuous learning is critical for keeping up with evolving crises, emerging best practices, and cross-cultural engagement. Equip field staff and program managers with the necessary technical and leadership skills to operate effectively in complex environments and mental health-focused training and mentoring programs can employees develop resilience, adaptability, and coping skills. Moreover, mentoring programs provide employees with support networks, offering guidance and career development opportunities and contributing to collaborative workplace communities.

Recommendations

Training

At the most basic level, every employee who joins the organization must be trained on the requirements of their position. Most organizations stop there, and dedicated employees are able to continue growing and learning on their own, but offering continuous opportunities  for training throughout their career will incentivize them to stay and grow with the organization.

If professional training courses are not within your budget, there are plenty of free resources available as well. However, don’t underestimate the potential of your own staff. Establishing peer training workshops is a great low-effort and low-cost way to train your staff while building community.

Education

While training focuses on hands-on skills-based development, education provides theoretical knowledge and insights, usually in an academic setting. Some employees may want to acquire additional degrees, and education reimbursement or tuition assistance programs are excellent ways to foster their curiosity and help them become more effective workers.

Other employees may be more interested in course- or certificate-based education which, just like training, is available as a paid or free service. Consider adding these to your virtual workspace and speak with your employees to understand their desire for continued education programs.

Mentorship

One-on-one coaching and mentorship offers employees individualized support and career guidance that helps them navigate career progression, develop leadership skills, and feel more connected to their work. Since every employee’s default mentor is their direct supervisor, ensure that all managers and supervisors receive training in mentorship so they can succeed in this role.

Beyond the default, creating mentorship programs that connect employees across different departments or even different organizations can create unique opportunities to gain new perspectives, enhance leadership skills, and build relationships that strengthen collaboration within the sector.

Like training and education, you can outsource mentorship if managers and supervisors lack the time or skills  to meet this need.
